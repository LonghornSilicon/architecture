Longhorn Accelerator for Matrix-Based Dataflow & Attention  ·  Chips, designed at Texas
Process: TSMC N16FFC  ·  Die: 2.0 × 2.0 mm = 4 mm²  ·  Off-chip: 1× LPDDR5X-8533 x16 (12 GB/s sustained)

Drawn at 250 pixels = 1 mm. Die is 2.0 × 2.0 mm = 4.0 mm² with a 100 µm I/O ring on every side (0.76 mm² of perimeter). All block sizes are area-faithful within ±5%; aspect ratios are PnR-suggestive but not binding (real placement determined by Innovus/IC Compiler II during PD). Numbers verified by scripts/v2_design_space/lambda_v2_audit.py.

What's on-die vs off-chip: the rectangle labeled "Lambda silicon" is the 4 mm² ASIC we tape out — it contains 8 functional blocks plus an I/O ring. The dark box below the die is the LPDDR5X-8533 DRAM package, a separate mobile-grade memory chip mounted on the same PCB next to Lambda. The "LPDDR5X x16 PHY" block on the bottom edge of our die is just the analog interface (data lanes, clocking, calibration) that drives signals out through pad-ring pins, across PCB traces, and into the off-chip DRAM package. The DRAM itself — 4–8 GB of storage holding your model weights — is not on Lambda's die.
Pivotal uncertainty (R-Lv2-03): the LPDDR5X x16 PHY is estimated at 1.20 mm² but the real range is 1.0–1.5 mm². At 1.0 (vendor best case): SRAM can grow to 1.0 MB. At 1.5 (worst case): chip doesn't fit at 4 mm² — must downgrade to LPDDR4X x16 (loses 4-5B model class) or grow die to 4.5 mm². Get written quote from Synopsys/Cadence Q2 2026 before floorplan freeze.

4. KV scratchpad capacity — long-context coverage

KV bytes per token per layer = kv_heads × head_dim × 2 (K+V) × 3.5 bits / 8 bits/byte. The 0.4 MB scratchpad is the largest single SRAM bank — explicitly KV-dominant per the agent thesis.

Streaming penalty when scratchpad doesn't cover full context: at 12 GB/s LPDDR sustained, reading 8K context's all-layer compressed KV adds only ~1-3 ms/token to decode (negligible vs ~125 ms decode). 32K adds ~5-10 ms. FP16 bypass1×Debug / ablation
Attention scoringINT8 Q × INT3 K → FP16 softmax accumulator—FlashAttention-3 mixed precision

Attention variants

  • Vanilla MHA — full multi-head attention (every Q has its own K, V)
  • GQA (Grouped Query Attention) — N query heads share M KV heads (Llama-3 family, Mistral-NeMo)
  • MQA (Multi-Query Attention) — degenerate GQA with 1 KV head; same hardware
  • FlashAttention-3 online softmax — VecU microcode running running (m_i, l_i, O_i) per query row
  • vLLM PagedAttention — MSC's 128-entry block table is the silicon implementation of vLLM's KV abstraction

Explicitly NOT supported in v2 baseline

  • Continuous batching (single-session chip — sized for batch 1)
  • Tier-3 host-DRAM eviction (no PCIe; no big-storage path)
  • Prefix sharing across sessions
  • Multi-Latent Attention (DeepSeek MLA — different KV layout)
  • Mixture of Experts routing
  • Multimodal (vision encoder, audio)
  • Training / backprop
  • Runtime DVFS / voltage scaling

v2 wins when: the team can absorb LPDDR PHY integration risk, demo story is "look, it runs Llama-3.2-3B standalone at 8 tok/s," and software engineering is NOT in place to build a coprocessor stack.

v1 wins when: software engineering IS in place (CPU runtime to integrate with), customer wedge is CPU-paired KV acceleration (llama.cpp users), or LPDDR PHY risk is unacceptable.

Drawn at 250 pixels = 1 mm. Die is 2.0 × 2.0 mm = 4.0 mm² with a 100 µm I/O ring on every side (0.76 mm² of perimeter). All block sizes are area-faithful within ±5%; aspect ratios are PnR-suggestive but not binding (real placement determined by Cadence Innovus during PD). Numbers verified by the 2026-05-14 area audit (see STATUS.md §4).

What's on-die vs off-chip: the rectangle labeled "Lambda silicon" is the 4 mm² ASIC we tape out — it contains 9 functional blocks (MatE, VecU, KCE-mini, MSC, LSU, TIU, HIF + SRAM banks + LPDDR PHY) plus an I/O ring. The dark box below the die is the LPDDR5X-8533 DRAM package, a separate mobile-grade memory chip mounted on the same PCB next to Lambda. The "LPDDR5X x16 PHY" block on the bottom edge of our die is just the analog interface (data lanes, clocking, calibration) that drives signals out through pad-ring pins, across PCB traces, and into the off-chip DRAM package. The DRAM itself — 4–8 GB of storage holding your model weights — is not on Lambda's die.
Pivotal uncertainty (R-Lv2-03): the LPDDR5X x16 PHY is estimated at 1.20 mm² but the real range is 1.0–1.5 mm². At 1.0 (vendor best case): SRAM can grow to 1.0 MB. At 1.5 (worst case): chip doesn't fit at 4 mm² — must either grow die to 4.5 mm² (~+12% shuttle), or fall back to LPDDR4X x16 (Cadence) at ~1.0 mm² PHY (loses ~half the bandwidth → caps at 2.4B models at 5 tok/s but recovers 0.2 mm² and gets a vendor with public datasheets). See STATUS.md for the full LPDDR5X-vs-LPDDR4X tradeoff. Get written quote from Synopsys/Cadence Q2 2026 before floorplan freeze.

4. KV scratchpad capacity — corrected 2026-05-14

KV bytes per token per layer = kv_heads × head_dim × 2 (K+V) × bpe / 8 bits/byte with bpe = 4.0 for v2 KCE-mini (16-pt Hadamard + 3-bit codebook + 16-bit FP16 scale per 16-element group). Earlier drafts of this document used 3.5 bpe (flagship 32-pt value) AND dropped the K+V doubling, producing capacity numbers ~2.3× too generous. Numbers below are correct.

What changed: earlier drafts claimed "Qwen2.5-3B 32K context fits ON-DIE per layer (4,681 tokens at 0.5 MB)". The 4,681 figure dropped the K+V factor of 2 AND used 3.5 bpe; with the K+V factor restored and v2's correct 4.0 bpe, only 2,048 tokens fit at 0.5 MB. 32K context does NOT fit on-die in any configuration — it serves via LPDDR streaming.

LPDDR KV-streaming penalty (per token, 4.0 bpe, 12 GB/s sustained):
  • Qwen2.5-3B (2 KV × 128, 36L): 4K ~3 ms, 8K ~6 ms, 32K ~24 ms → 32K end-to-end ~6.5 tok/s (above threshold)
  • Llama-3.2-3B (8 KV × 128, 28L): 4K ~9 ms, 8K ~19 ms, 32K ~75 ms → 32K drops to ~4.9 tok/s (at threshold)
  • Mistral-NeMo-3B: identical to Llama-3.2-3B
  • Phi-3.5-mini (32 KV × 96, 32L): 4K ~33 ms — even at 4K, decode drops below 5 tok/s. Not recommended as demo target.
Headline demo guidance: keep target prompts ≤ 8K for comfortable interactive operation across all 3B-class targets. Qwen2.5-3B is the long-context demo champ (sustainable 32K via streaming).

Attention variants

  • Vanilla MHA — full multi-head attention (every Q has its own K, V)
  • GQA (Grouped Query Attention) — N query heads share M KV heads (Llama-3 family, Mistral-NeMo)
  • MQA (Multi-Query Attention) — degenerate GQA with 1 KV head; same hardware
  • FlashAttention-3 online softmax — VecU microcode running running (m_i, l_i, O_i) per query row
  • vLLM PagedAttention — MSC's 128-entry block table is the silicon implementation of vLLM's KV abstraction

Explicitly NOT supported in v2 baseline

  • Continuous batching (single-session chip — sized for batch 1)
  • Tier-3 host-DRAM eviction (no PCIe; no big-storage path)
  • Prefix sharing across sessions
  • Multi-Latent Attention (DeepSeek MLA — different KV layout)
  • Mixture of Experts routing
  • Multimodal (vision encoder, audio)
  • Training / backprop
  • Runtime DVFS / voltage scaling

8. Audit log (2026-05-14)

The 2026-05-14 pre-RTL audit caught the following bugs in this spec and corrected them:

  1. KV bytes/token off by ~2.3×. Spec dropped the K+V factor of 2 AND used flagship's 3.5 bpe instead of v2 KCE-mini's correct 4.0 bpe. Re-derived all capacity numbers in Panel 4.
  2. "Qwen2.5-3B 32K on-die" claim was wrong. Even with the most aggressive shrinks, 32K does not fit on-die per layer (would need ~8 MB/layer). Reframed: 32K is serviceable via LPDDR streaming at ~24 ms/tok overhead → 6.5 tok/s sustained. Qwen2.5-3B remains the best long-context target on Lambda.
  3. "INT16 accumulator" in MatE was a real bug. INT8 × INT4 → 11-bit signed product; reducing K=128 needs 18 bits — INT16 saturates after ~64 accumulations in the worst case. Corrected to INT24 K-axis accumulator (with INT16 partial-product register inside each PE).
  4. "3-5× compute headroom" claim was wrong. In the bandwidth-bound regime, GOPS_needed scales with bandwidth (not model size), so headroom is constant 1.6× across all reasonable models.
  5. "Comfortable headroom at PHY=1.0 mm²" was misleading. The sensitivity sweep shows +0.002 mm² headroom — exactly fits, no margin.
  6. weight_stream_buffer was inconsistent (0.05 vs 0.15 MB). Canonical value: 0.05 MB (40× LPDDR latency-hiding minimum — ample).
  7. Total SRAM was inconsistent (0.8 / 0.85 / 1.0 MB). Canonical baseline: 0.8 MB at PHY=1.2 mm²; upgradable to 1.0 MB if PHY lands ≤ 1.0 mm².
  8. KCE-mini compression mode bpe inconsistent (3.5 / 4.0 / 5.3). Canonical: 4.0 bpe for primary 3-bit + Hadamard-16 mode → 4.0× vs FP16.

No architectural changes — every fix was either a math error, a mis-derivation, or a stale claim from an earlier iteration. Full audit log in STATUS.md.

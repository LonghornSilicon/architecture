\begin{abstract}
\boldmath
We present \textbf{Lambda}, a 4\,mm$^2$ standalone transformer-decode ASIC at TSMC N16FFC, designed for tape-out via the IMEC / Europractice mini@sic 2.0 academic shuttle program. Lambda runs 3--5\,B-parameter W4A8 transformer models---Llama-3.2-3B, Mistral-NeMo-3B, Qwen2.5-3B---at 6--8\,tokens/s decode in a 2.6\,W typical envelope, on a single LPDDR5X-8533 x16 channel sustaining 12\,GB/s. Three architectural contributions distinguish the chip. \textbf{(1)} We implement TurboQuant rotation-codebook KV compression in silicon at 4.0 effective bits per element (4.0$\times$ vs.\ FP16), via a 16-point Walsh--Hadamard butterfly and 8-centroid Lloyd--Max codebook---a zero-multiplier compression engine occupying 0.08\,mm$^2$. \textbf{(2)} We perform compressed-domain attention scoring directly on 3-bit codebook indices (INT8\,$\times$\,INT3 with an INT24 reduction along the head dimension), eliminating the need for an FP16 multiplier fabric. \textbf{(3)} We introduce a Token Importance Unit (TIU): a 0.03\,mm$^2$ block that accumulates per-block attention entropy and drives both heavy-hitter eviction and adaptive per-block precision in the KV cache. The complete chip integrates seven functional units under an ACU/MSC/LSU/TIU/HIF top-level hierarchy and presents a PCIe Gen3 x1 host interface on an M.2 2280 form factor. We document the design-space exploration that led to the 4\,mm$^2$ target, the LPDDR5X-vs-LPDDR4X PHY tradeoff that gates the model-class commitment, and an explicit pre-RTL audit of the spec that corrected eight numerical inconsistencies before any high-level synthesis work begins. Lambda is, to our knowledge, the first open-source academic standalone transformer accelerator targeting the 3--5\,B-parameter on-device deployment regime.
\unboldmath
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
% =====================================================================

The 3--5-billion-parameter transformer has become the practical on-device deployment regime for large language models in 2025--2026. Llama-3.2-3B~\cite{llama3}, Mistral-NeMo-3B~\cite{mistral_nemo}, Qwen2.5-3B~\cite{qwen25}, Phi-3.5-mini~\cite{phi35}, and Gemma-2-2B all target this band; Apple's recently-published foundation-language-model family~\cite{apple_foundation}, Google Gemini Nano, and Microsoft Copilot+ all deploy at similar scales. The accelerators that serve these workloads in production---Apple's Neural Engine, Qualcomm's Hexagon NPU---are proprietary integrated SoCs whose internal architectures are not publicly documented. Modern GPU accelerators~\cite{nvidia_ampere,nvidia_hopper,nvidia_blackwell} serve the workload at the data-center scale but at die areas and power envelopes 10$^2$--10$^3$ times Lambda's. No open-source academic standalone accelerator at this model class and process node currently exists.

Lambda fills that gap. We present a 4\,mm$^2$ ASIC at TSMC N16FFC, designed for tape-out through the IMEC / Europractice mini@sic 2.0 academic shuttle program~\cite{imec_minisic} at a shuttle cost of approximately \$60--100\,K. The chip runs a 3-billion-parameter W4A8 transformer at 6--8 decode tokens per second in a 2.6\,W typical envelope, holding 4--8\,GB of weights in an off-chip LPDDR5X-8533 x16 package and exposing host I/O over a PCIe Gen3 x1 link on an M.2 2280 carrier card.

Three architectural commitments differentiate Lambda from prior open-source accelerator designs:

\textbf{(1) Rotation-codebook KV compression in silicon.} Lambda's KV-compression engine (KCE-mini) implements TurboQuant~\cite{turboquant}---a 2025 algorithm combining Walsh--Hadamard rotation, Lloyd--Max scalar codebook quantization, and per-group magnitude scaling---in a 0.08\,mm$^2$ block that operates without multipliers on either the encode or decode path. At our 16-point Hadamard size, the effective compression is 4.0 bits per element, a 4.0$\times$ reduction versus FP16, with no observable quality loss on LongBench~\cite{turboquant} or Needle-in-a-Haystack benchmarks. We believe Lambda is the first silicon implementation of TurboQuant at a competitive process node.

\textbf{(2) Compressed-domain attention scoring.} Because TurboQuant's Hadamard rotation spreads outlier coordinates across all dimensions, the attention dot product Q\,$\cdot$\,K$^\top$ can be computed directly on the 3-bit compressed codebook indices and rescaled once at the row sum. Lambda's matrix engine (MatE) therefore needs no FP16 multiplier datapath; an INT8\,$\times$\,INT3 multiplier with an INT24 K-axis accumulator suffices for attention scoring against the full KV cache. This eliminates approximately 0.4\,mm$^2$ of FP16 fabric that comparable mixed-precision designs (e.g.,~\cite{adaptive_kv}) require.

\textbf{(3) Token Importance Unit (TIU) for adaptive precision.} Inspired by the entropy-driven KV quantization analysis of~\cite{adaptive_kv}, we introduce a small (0.03\,mm$^2$) on-die accumulator that tracks the cumulative softmax weight per 16-token KV block. The TIU output drives two consumer paths: a heavy-hitter eviction policy in the memory subsystem controller (H2O-style retention~\cite{h2o}) and a per-block precision selector that lets the KCE downgrade low-importance blocks to 3.0 or 2.0 bits per element. To our knowledge, this is the first silicon implementation of adaptive-precision KV cache management; published prior work is software-only.

The remainder of this paper develops these contributions in detail. Section~\ref{sec:background} surveys the prior art the design is grounded in. Section~\ref{sec:architecture} describes Lambda's seven functional blocks and their top-level grouping. Section~\ref{sec:dataflow} traces the decode token data flow and the quantization decisions that shape it. Section~\ref{sec:implementation} reports the area and power budgets, including a pre-RTL audit that corrected eight numerical inconsistencies in the original spec. Section~\ref{sec:performance} characterizes Lambda's coverage across the candidate-model space and quantifies the load-bearing LPDDR5X-vs-LPDDR4X PHY tradeoff. Section~\ref{sec:discussion} discusses what we deliberately excluded and why.

\section{Background and Related Work}
\label{sec:background}
% =====================================================================

Lambda integrates four lines of recent research into a single chip target.

\textbf{Online attention.} FlashAttention~\cite{flashattention,flashattention2,flashattention3} reformulates softmax as an online tiled reduction with per-row running maxima $m_i$ and running sums-of-exponentials $\ell_i$. This eliminates materialization of the score matrix and reduces the activation-memory pressure that dominates long-context attention. Lambda's vector unit (VecU) implements the FlashAttention-3 online softmax algorithm in microcode; the running state per row is two FP16 scalars plus the running output accumulator $O_i$. VecU also implements the rotary positional embedding scheme (RoPE)~\cite{rope}, RMSNorm~\cite{rmsnorm}, and the SwiGLU gating used by the Llama-3 family~\cite{swiglu} as microcoded loops.

\textbf{Paged KV management.} vLLM's PagedAttention~\cite{pagedattention} treats the KV cache as a virtual address space with fixed-size physical blocks, indexed by a per-sequence block table. The pattern enables zero-fragmentation memory use and dynamic eviction. Lambda's memory subsystem controller (MSC) implements this in silicon as a 128-entry content-addressable block table with 16-token blocks, the silicon analog of vLLM's logical-to-physical mapping. FlashInfer~\cite{flashinfer} extends the paged abstraction to a composable taxonomy of attention patterns (dense paged, sparse structured, ragged, composite); Lambda's MSC exposes a CSR-selectable mode covering this taxonomy.

\textbf{Weight and activation quantization.} W4A8 has emerged as the production sweet spot for small-LLM inference~\cite{w4a8_acl25}: 4-bit weights (via GPTQ~\cite{gptq} or AWQ~\cite{awq}) and 8-bit per-token dynamic activations (via SmoothQuant~\cite{smoothquant}) preserve quality on the 3--5\,B class within a few MMLU points of FP16. Lambda's MatE multiplies INT8 activations against INT4 weights as its primary path.

\textbf{KV-cache compression.} A growing body of work~\cite{turboquant,kvquant,kv_survey,quarot,rotatekv} compresses KV caches via uniform quantization, rotation, codebook quantization, or per-token mixed precision. TurboQuant~\cite{turboquant} combines Walsh--Hadamard rotation (which flattens outliers into a roughly uniform distribution) with an 8-centroid Lloyd--Max codebook at 3-bit indices and per-group magnitude scale. At a 32-point Hadamard the effective rate is 3.5 bits per element (4.57$\times$ vs.\ FP16); at the 16-point variant we adopt for area, the rate is 4.0 bits per element (4.0$\times$). The choice of rotation-codebook quantization is consequential for the rest of the chip: the resulting K vectors have no outlier coordinates, so attention scoring at INT8\,$\times$\,INT3 (compressed-domain) is quality-neutral and the FP16 fallback paths that adaptive-precision designs~\cite{adaptive_kv} otherwise need can be omitted.

\textbf{Attention-weight-driven eviction and adaptive precision.} H2O~\cite{h2o}, Scissorhands~\cite{scissorhands}, TOVA~\cite{tova}, and the StreamingLLM ``attention-sink'' phenomenon~\cite{streamingllm} all show that retaining tokens whose cumulative attention weight exceeds a threshold (and evicting the rest) preserves long-context quality at a fraction of the KV-cache footprint. The recent adaptive-precision-KV work~\cite{adaptive_kv} extends this idea to bit-width allocation: high-attention tokens stay at higher precision, low-attention tokens demote. Lambda's TIU is the silicon expression of this idea at per-block granularity (16 tokens per block, matching the PagedAttention block size).

\subsection{Matrix Engine (MatE)}

MatE is an 8$\times$8 weight-stationary systolic array of 64 INT8\,$\times$\,INT4 processing elements (PEs). At 1\,GHz, peak throughput is 128\,GOPS; sustained throughput at 60\,\% utilization is 76.8\,GOPS---a figure that is, in the bandwidth-bound regime, 1.6$\times$ the throughput the chip needs at the largest model size. The factor is constant across model sizes in this regime because the GOPS demand scales with bandwidth (not with model size); we found and corrected an earlier draft claim of ``3--5$\times$ headroom'' as a derivation error during the spec audit.

Two dataflow modes are CSR-selectable:

\textit{Weight-stationary} for the GEMMs that dominate transformer compute: Q/K/V projections, attention output projection, FFN up- and down-projections, and the final logits projection. Weights pin once per tile; activations stream from the activation buffer; products accumulate down the columns.

\textit{Output-stationary} for the Q\,$\cdot$\,K$^\top$ attention scoring step. Q vectors pin for the duration of the attention tile; compressed K values stream from the kv\_scratchpad. Because K is stored in TurboQuant 3-bit codebook indices, MatE multiplies INT8\,$\times$\,INT3 directly---no dequantization to FP16 is required. The 11-bit signed products feed an INT24 reduction along the head-dimension axis; a single FP16 dequantization scale is applied once at the row sum. We selected INT24 (rather than the INT16 the original spec contained) after analysis showed that reducing $K{=}128$ INT8\,$\times$\,INT4 products requires $\log_2(128) + 11 = 18$ signed bits in the worst case---comfortably outside INT16's range. The INT16 width survives only as the partial-product register inside each PE, following the per-PE/per-column accumulator split of Jouppi's TPU~\cite{jouppi_tpu,tpu_v4}.

\subsection{Vector Unit (VecU)}

VecU is eight FP16/BF16 SIMD lanes sharing a 1\,K-instruction microcode RAM and three 64-entry transcendental LUTs (\texttt{exp}, \texttt{rsqrt}, \texttt{sigmoid}) with linear interpolation. The unit handles every non-GEMM operation in the transformer: FlashAttention-3 online softmax, rotary positional embedding (RoPE) pair-rotation, RMSNorm, SiLU/GELU, residual addition, and sampling. We chose programmable SIMD over fixed-function blocks for verification economy---one microcoded block has a smaller verification surface than four fixed-function blocks.

The online softmax microcode is the most consequential program. Per attention row, VecU maintains a triple $(m_i, \ell_i, O_i)$: running max, running sum-of-exponentials, and running output accumulator. As each tile of scores arrives, VecU computes the new max, rescales the running accumulators by $e^{m_{\mathrm{old}} - m_{\mathrm{new}}}$, exponentiates the tile via the exp LUT, and accumulates into $\ell_i$ and into $O_i$ weighted by the corresponding V tile. The 8 lanes parallelize across rows; tile size is 32--64 scores; approximately 32 microinstructions execute per tile.

\subsection{KV Compression Engine (KCE-mini)}

KCE-mini is the headline research IP. At 0.08\,mm$^2$, it implements TurboQuant compression and decompression on 16-element groups via three stages: (i)\ a 16-point Walsh--Hadamard butterfly (4 stages of 8 add/subtract operations each, 64 operations total) which rotates the input into a basis where outlier coordinates are spread roughly uniformly; (ii)\ an 8-centroid Lloyd--Max nearest-centroid classifier (7 comparators per lane $\times$ 16 lanes = 112 comparators) which assigns each rotated coordinate to a 3-bit codebook index; and (iii)\ bit-packing with a 16-bit FP16 per-group magnitude scale. The effective rate is $(16 \times 3 + 16) / 16 = 4.0$ bits per element---a 4.0$\times$ compression versus FP16.

Critically, neither the encode nor the decode path contains a multiplier. The Hadamard butterfly is sign-pattern add/subtract; the codebook classifier is comparison-based; the decode path is a LUT read plus an inverse Hadamard. This is what allows KCE-mini to fit in 0.08\,mm$^2$ at our node. Five CSR-selectable modes provide design flexibility: the primary TurboQuant 3-bit + 16-point mode, a linear Hadamard-INT4 fallback, an asymmetric K-3-bit/V-2-bit production mode that yields 4.57$\times$ compression on average, an FP4 (E2M1) codebook mode using NVFP4 levels~\cite{nvfp4,nvidia_blackwell} or OCP Microscaling MXFP4~\cite{ocp_mx} via an alternative ROM, and an FP16 bypass for ablation.

We caught and corrected three inconsistent claims about KCE-mini's compression rate during the pre-RTL audit. Earlier spec drafts variously claimed 3.5 bits/element (the flagship 32-point Hadamard rate), 5.3 bits/element (apparently including unspecified extra overhead), and 3.0$\times$ compression. The 4.0 bits/element figure derives directly from first principles for the 16-point variant and is the canonical value.

\subsection{Memory Subsystem Controller (MSC)}

MSC integrates four functions in 0.18\,mm$^2$: the LPDDR5X x16 protocol-side controller (with open-page policy and bank-conflict avoidance), a 4-port SRAM crossbar serving MatE, VecU, KCE, and the host, a 128-entry block table providing the silicon analog of vLLM's PagedAttention~\cite{pagedattention} indexing (16 tokens per block, 2\,K addressable tokens per session), and a DMA descriptor FSM. Sparse-blocked attention is exposed as a CSR-selectable mode (\texttt{attention\_pattern\_modes}) following the FlashInfer taxonomy~\cite{flashinfer}: dense paged, sliding window, sparse blocked, local+global hybrid (the Gemma-2 layout).

MSC is single-session by design. We exclude continuous batching (multi-session state), tier-3 host-DRAM eviction (we have no PCIe-to-host swap path on a 4\,mm$^2$ die), and cross-session prefix sharing. These omissions are deliberate: the chip is sized for batch-1 on-device inference, not multi-tenant serving.

\subsection{Layer Sequencer (LSU)}

The LSU is a minimal three-stage in-order RISC: 32-bit fixed-width instruction format, 32 instructions, 16 $\times$ 32-bit general-purpose registers, 4\,KB of microcode RAM holding the entire pre-compiled per-layer schedule, single-issue across three dispatch lanes (scalar, vector to ACU, DMA to MSC). No branch predictor, no out-of-order machinery, no cache hierarchy: transformer decode is structurally identical layer to layer, and a static schedule walked deterministically suffices. The host loads roughly 3\,K microcode instructions at boot; the chip executes the same schedule forever. The footprint is 0.10\,mm$^2$.

\subsection{Token Importance Unit (TIU)}

TIU is a 0.03\,mm$^2$ block introduced in this work. It maintains a 256-byte importance SRAM organized as 128 entries of 16 bits each (one entry per PagedAttention block). Update path: VecU broadcasts the per-block cumulative softmax weight of the current attention pass; the TIU adds to the corresponding importance register. Consumer paths: (i) MSC queries the lowest-importance block when the kv\_scratchpad approaches capacity (heavy-hitter eviction, H2O-style~\cite{h2o}), and (ii) KCE-mini queries the per-block importance bucket when re-compressing an evicted-and-recalled block, dropping low-importance blocks to 3.0 or 2.0 bits per element while keeping high-importance blocks at the 4.0\,bpe primary mode.

Four CSR-selectable policies cover the design space: \texttt{tiu\_off} (pure FIFO eviction, uniform precision), \texttt{tiu\_h2o} (heavy-hitter retention, uniform precision), \texttt{tiu\_streaming\_llm} (recent + attention-sink retention), and \texttt{tiu\_adaptive\_precision} (heavy-hitter retention plus per-block adaptive precision). The block is grounded in the adaptive-precision-KV analysis of~\cite{adaptive_kv}, which we believe has not previously been implemented in silicon.

\subsection{Host Interface (HIF)}

HIF is a PCIe Gen3 x1 endpoint (~1\,GB/s sustained) on the M.2 2280 form factor at the PCB level. The M.2 connector wires four PCIe lanes by specification; Lambda drives only one on-die, and PCIe link training negotiates the link down to x1. Vendor IP (Synopsys DesignWare or Cadence's PCIe Gen3 PHY at 16\,nm) provides the analog and protocol stack; we implement the BAR0 CSR map, the 16-deep doorbell command queue, and the JTAG TAP. JTAG runs on dedicated pins, separate from the PCIe lanes, so post-silicon debug remains accessible before PCIe link is up. Total block area is 0.55\,mm$^2$, of which the vendor PHY accounts for approximately 0.35\,mm$^2$.

We initially specified USB-C 2.0 for this interface and revised to PCIe Gen3 x1 mid-design after concluding that the 25-second weight-load latency of USB-C 2.0 was a development-iteration cost we did not want to accept, and that the M.2 form factor better matched the modern inference-card target. The PCIe vendor IP also has public 16\,nm datasheets, in contrast to the LPDDR5X x16 PHY which remains NDA-gated at 16\,nm; the PCIe-side risk profile is therefore lower.

\section{Dataflow and Quantization}
\label{sec:dataflow}
% =====================================================================

We walk one decode token of a 3-billion-parameter model (Llama-3.2-3B, 28 layers, 8 KV heads of 128 dimensions, 3072 hidden width, FFN width 8192) through the pipeline to make the quantization commitments concrete.

The LSU issues approximately 200 microinstructions per layer. For each layer, the schedule alternates four operand-stationary patterns. \textbf{(1) Q/K/V projection:} MatE consumes the activation vector and three weight matrices (streamed from LPDDR via the weight\_stream\_buffer), in weight-stationary mode, producing three 1024-dimensional output vectors. \textbf{(2) RoPE rotation:} VecU rotates Q and K pair-wise using the rotary frequency table. \textbf{(3) KV compression:} KCE-mini compresses the new K and V into 16-element groups, writing 3-bit codebook indices plus per-group FP16 scales into the kv\_scratchpad. \textbf{(4) Attention scoring:} MatE switches to output-stationary mode and computes Q\,$\cdot$\,K$^\top$ \textit{against the compressed K values directly}. The multiplier path is INT8 (Q) $\times$ INT3 (codebook index), the partial product is 11-bit signed, the K-axis reduction is INT24, and the per-group FP16 scale is applied once at the row sum. K is never expanded back to FP16.

The scores feed VecU, which executes the FlashAttention-3 online softmax microcode in tiles of 32. Simultaneously, the running output accumulator $O_i$ is updated by multiplying scaled scores against the (compressed) V values through the same MatE path. When the last tile is consumed, $O_i$ is the attention output. The TIU has been quietly accumulating the per-block cumulative softmax weight throughout this loop; the importance SRAM is now ready for the next eviction or precision-tuning decision.

The post-attention path runs through MatE again (attention output projection, FFN up-projection, FFN down-projection) and VecU (SiLU, residual add, RMSNorm). End-to-end, the schedule completes one layer in approximately 4.5\,ms at 3-billion parameters on LPDDR5X x16; 28 layers compose a 125\,ms-per-token decode cadence, or 8 tokens per second. Compute is bandwidth-bound throughout: the limiting factor is the time required to stream the weights of each linear layer from LPDDR.

\subsection{Why no FP16 multiplier in MatE?}

A common alternative approach---adopted, for example, in adaptive-precision-attention designs~\cite{adaptive_kv,chaithu_acu}---routes ``outlier'' attention tiles to an FP16 multiplier and ``inlier'' tiles to an INT8 multiplier, with a per-tile gating function deciding which path to take. The design rationale is that some attention tiles are dominated by a few high-magnitude scores that would lose precision under INT8 quantization.

TurboQuant's Hadamard rotation makes this gating unnecessary. The rotation spreads outlier coordinates across all dimensions, producing a roughly uniform distribution. After rotation, every coordinate is amenable to the same 3-bit codebook quantization; no tile is an ``outlier'' tile in the relevant sense. The empirical evidence is in the TurboQuant paper~\cite{turboquant}: quality is preserved on LongBench and Needle-in-a-Haystack across Gemma and Mistral families at 3.5\,bpe (32-point) and degrades only modestly at lower bpe. We rely on this property to omit the FP16 multiplier from MatE entirely, saving approximately 0.4\,mm$^2$ of fabric area at our node. An FP16 path remains in VecU, where it is needed for softmax, RMSNorm, RoPE, and other transcendental-heavy operations.

\subsection{Pre-RTL audit}

Before any HLS work begins we ran an explicit numerical audit of the spec and corrected eight inconsistencies. We document the audit here because each correction is a load-bearing decision that the rest of the chip integrates over.

\textit{KV bytes per token were 2.3$\times$ understated.} The bytes-per-token-per-layer formula in the original spec used $k_h \times d_h \times \mathrm{bpe}/8$, dropping the factor of 2 for K and V separately, and used the flagship 3.5\,bpe figure rather than our 16-point variant's 4.0\,bpe. We re-derived every capacity number against the correct $k_h \times d_h \times 2 \times \mathrm{bpe}/8$ formula at 4.0\,bpe.

\textit{The ``Qwen2.5-3B 32K on-die'' claim was wrong.} With corrected math, no 3-billion-parameter target model holds 32\,K context fully on-die per layer; the 0.4\,MB scratchpad holds 1638 tokens for the 2-KV-head Qwen layout and 409 tokens for the 8-KV-head Llama/Mistral layouts. Long contexts serve via LPDDR streaming, at an overhead of approximately 24\,ms per token at 32\,K for the Qwen layout (still above the 5\,tok/s threshold) and approximately 75\,ms per token for the 8-head layouts (below threshold).

\textit{MatE's INT16 accumulator was a real bug.} The original spec specified INT16 K-axis accumulators ``because 3--5B models tolerate it.'' INT8\,$\times$\,INT4 produces an 11-bit signed product; reducing $K{=}128$ requires 18 signed bits, saturating INT16 after approximately 64 accumulations in the worst case. Corrected to INT24 with an INT16 partial-product register inside each PE. The model class does not bear on the accumulator width.

\textit{The ``3--5$\times$ compute headroom'' claim was a derivation error.} In the bandwidth-bound regime, $\mathrm{GOPS}_{\mathrm{needed}} = 2 \cdot \mathrm{BW}_{\mathrm{GB/s}} / \mathrm{bytes}_{\mathrm{per\ param}}$, a constant across model sizes (because both numerator and denominator scale with bandwidth). The correct figure is a constant 1.6$\times$ headroom.

Additional corrections: the ``comfortable headroom at PHY = 1.0\,mm$^2$'' claim was misleading (the sensitivity sweep actually returns $+0.002$\,mm$^2$ at 1.0\,MB SRAM---exactly fits, no margin); the weight\_stream\_buffer was 0.05/0.15\,MB inconsistent (canonical 0.05); the total SRAM was 0.8/0.85/1.0\,MB inconsistent (canonical 0.8); the KCE-mini effective rate was 3.5/4.0/5.3\,bpe inconsistent (canonical 4.0).

None of the corrections changed any architectural decision. Every fix was either a math error or a stale claim from an earlier iteration. We document them here because pre-RTL audits of this kind are, in our experience, frequently skipped in academic chip projects and frequently catch real bugs.

\subsection{The LPDDR5X-vs-LPDDR4X PHY tradeoff}

The dominant uncertainty in Lambda's area budget is the LPDDR5X x16 PHY footprint at 16\,nm, which is NDA-gated and which we estimate at 1.20\,mm$^2$ with $\pm 0.3$\,mm$^2$ real swing. The estimate is extrapolated from vendor x64 PHY data points; an authoritative number requires a written Synopsys or Cadence quote in Q2 2026. If the quote returns above 1.35\,mm$^2$, Lambda's recommended shrink path no longer closes at 4\,mm$^2$, and a fallback is required.

The natural fallback is LPDDR4X x16 with a Cadence PHY. LPDDR4X has been shipping at 16\,nm in multiple commercial parts since approximately 2018 and has public datasheets, in contrast to LPDDR5X's NDA-only 16\,nm references. The Cadence PHY for LPDDR4X at 16\,nm is approximately 1.0\,mm$^2$ with substantially less area variance ($\pm 0.15$\,mm$^2$). Adopting LPDDR4X frees approximately 0.2\,mm$^2$ of breathing room and materially de-risks the first-FinFET-PHY integration that is the chip's largest non-architectural risk.

The cost is bandwidth. LPDDR4X x16 sustains approximately 6\,GB/s, halving the largest serviceable model class from approximately 4.8\,B parameters to approximately 2.4\,B at the 5\,tok/s threshold. Llama-3.2-3B and Mistral-NeMo-3B drop below threshold; Llama-3.2-1B (9.6\,tok/s), Qwen2.5-1.5B (8.0\,tok/s), SmolLM2-1.7B (7.1\,tok/s), and Gemma-2-2B (6.0\,tok/s, borderline) remain comfortably above. The chip's research thesis shifts from ``first open-source academic standalone 3--5\,B accelerator at 16\,nm'' to ``first open-source academic standalone 1--2\,B accelerator at 16\,nm, with fully public-datasheet PHY IP''---a defensible alternative claim at materially lower tape-out risk.

The recommendation in our current planning is to commission both PHY quotes in parallel and gate the decision on the data. If the LPDDR5X x16 quote returns at or below 1.25\,mm$^2$ and a senior FinFET PD engineer is in place, the LPDDR5X path is correct; if the quote exceeds 1.35\,mm$^2$ or the PD-engineer prerequisite is not met, the LPDDR4X path is correct. Neither outcome kills the project.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
% =====================================================================

\subsection{What we deliberately excluded}

Lambda omits several mechanisms that are present in software-side frontier work. We document the exclusions because the omissions are deliberate and reversible in a future revision.

\textit{Multi-head Latent Attention (MLA).} DeepSeek-V2's MLA~\cite{mla} compresses Q/K/V into a low-rank latent space before the head split, reducing per-layer KV by approximately an order of magnitude versus standard MHA. Lambda's MSC and KCE-mini both assume the conventional $k_h \times d_h$ KV layout. MLA would require a redesign of both blocks; we defer it for a future revision and document the gap explicitly.

\textit{Continuous batching and prefix sharing.} The single-session chip excludes multi-tenant serving primitives. We exclude them deliberately: a 4\,mm$^2$ die does not have area for the multi-session state, and the on-device deployment target does not benefit from them.

\textit{Mixture-of-Experts.} MoE routing requires a sparse-dispatch fabric that does not fit our area budget and that is not yet a dominant pattern in the 3--5\,B class.

\textit{Mamba and other SSM blocks.} Selective state-space models~\cite{mamba} require different primitive operations from attention; the chip is not architected for them.

\textit{Multimodal vision/audio encoders.} Out of scope for the chip's text-only inference target.

\textit{Hardware speculative-decoding fabric.} Speculative decoding (Medusa~\cite{medusa}, EAGLE~\cite{eagle}) and multi-token prediction (DeepSeek-V3~\cite{deepseek_v3}) are architecturally compatible: the host coordinates draft tokens, and the chip runs the verifier pass through a standard batched-$N$ decode schedule. We do not include a dedicated speculative-decoding accelerator on-die; the LSU schedule is general enough to accommodate the pattern.

\subsection{IP clearance: the Etched patent}

Etched.ai's US patent 2024/0419516\,A1~\cite{etched_patent} describes an AI hardware platform comprising two structurally separated circuits on a single IC: a systolic array for operations that do not consume past-token data and a self-attention circuit for operations that do. Lambda's MatE is a \textit{single} 8$\times$8 systolic that mode-multiplexes between weight-stationary (for the no-past-token-data operations the patent describes as belonging to its systolic array) and output-stationary (for the past-token-data operations the patent describes as belonging to its self-attention circuit). The structural separation that grounds the patent's claim is absent in Lambda's design; the mode-switch is a CSR bit, not two circuits.

Our reading is that Lambda is outside the claim scope, but a defensive review with our institutional technology transfer office is the appropriate next step before silicon commit. The patent does highlight a real scheduling distinction we adopt explicitly: the LSU schedule treats weight-matmul tiles and attention-matmul tiles as separate dispatch primitives with separate optimization targets (K-axis size, operand stationarity).

\subsection{Risks and open questions}

The largest non-architectural risk is the LPDDR5X x16 PHY integration: this is the team's first FinFET-era PHY tape-out, and the 1.20\,mm$^2$ estimate has $\pm 0.3$\,mm$^2$ real swing. Mitigation is the vendor quote (Q2 2026) and a senior FinFET physical-design engineer in place by Q3 2026; the LPDDR4X fallback (Section~\ref{sec:performance}) is the documented release valve.

The IMEC mini@sic 2.0 actual pricing for 4\,mm$^2$ N16FFC is non-public; the \$60--100\,K range is an academic-discount-tier estimate. A direct quote via \texttt{eptsmc@imec.be} is the first concrete action item on the project's gantt.

\section{Conclusion}
% =====================================================================

We have presented Lambda, a 4\,mm$^2$ open-source academic transformer-decode ASIC at TSMC N16FFC that serves the 3--5\,B-parameter on-device deployment regime at 6--8\,tok/s in 2.6\,W. The chip is, to our knowledge, the first silicon implementation of TurboQuant rotation-codebook KV compression at a competitive process node, the first silicon implementation of adaptive-precision KV cache management via on-die attention-entropy accumulation (the TIU), and the first open-source academic standalone accelerator targeting this model class. We have documented the design-space exploration, the LPDDR5X-versus-LPDDR4X PHY tradeoff that gates the model-class commitment, and an explicit pre-RTL audit that corrected eight numerical inconsistencies before any HLS work begins. The next phase of the project is high-level synthesis of the MatE processing element and the KCE-mini Hadamard butterfly---the two long poles of the implementation effort. The full architecture specification, the audit log, the literature review, and the per-block HLS scaffolding are publicly available at \texttt{github.com/LonghornSilicon/architecture}.

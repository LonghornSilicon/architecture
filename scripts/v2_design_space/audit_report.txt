--- Conclusion ---
• Qwen2.5-3B is the long-context champion: 2 KV heads × 128 dim → 32K context
  fits in 0.5 MB scratchpad PER LAYER. No LPDDR KV streaming needed.
• Llama-3.2-3B and Mistral-NeMo (8 KV × 128 dim): ~470 single-layer tokens
  in 0.5 MB. KV streams from LPDDR for >1K contexts; ~1.7 ms/token at 4K.
• Phi-3.5-mini (32 KV × 96 dim): KV-heavy. Only ~190 single-layer tokens fit.
  4K context costs ~3.5 ms/token KV streaming; still acceptable.
• Asymmetric K3/V2 mode (~6× compression vs FP16) extends scratchpad capacity
  by 1.4×. Free upgrade — costs only an alt codebook ROM and 1 CSR mode bit.

• LPDDR power is the second-largest after leakage; PHY efficiency matters
  most when sponsor cares about battery life (mobile/edge deployment)
